Matthew J. Olson

Matthew J. Olson serves as Director of Choral Activities at Carleton College (MN) where he leads the choirs, instructs voice lessons, and teaches courses in conducting and a cappella arranging. Formerly, he served as Associate Conductor of The Singers – Minnesota Choral Artists, and founding Artistic Director of Bach Roots Festival whose performances of major choral-orchestral works were hailed as “masterful” and “gripping” (The Minnesota Star Tribune).

In his role with The Singers, he served as chorusmaster with The St. Paul Chamber Orchestra on multiple collaborations including performances of Bach’s St. John Passion described as “one of the most satisfying you could ever hope to hear” with a chorus “scrupulously prepared.” (Star Tribune) A passionate music educator, Matthew often leads choir festivals and recently served as a Visiting Professor at St. Olaf College and a guest conductor at Florida State University’s College of Music.

In 2024, he was named Minnesota’s Collegiate Music Educator of the Year. His award-winning compositions and arrangements are published by Colla Voce, Santa Barbara, and via his website www.matthewjolson.com. He studied choral and orchestral conducting at St. Olaf College, The Oregon Bach Festival, Michigan State University, The Canford Conducting School (U.K.), and The University of Minnesota. Matthew belongs to Beyond Artists, a coalition of artists that donates a percentage of their concert fees to non-profits they care about. He proudly supports College Possible.
